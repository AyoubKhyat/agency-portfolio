## A Market in Full Explosion

E-commerce in Morocco is experiencing unprecedented growth. According to the latest figures from the FNEM, the market has exceeded **20 billion dirhams** in 2025, with annual growth of over 30%. And this is just the beginning.

The pandemic accelerated a movement already underway: Moroccans are buying more and more online. From clothing to cosmetics, electronics to food, all sectors are affected.

## Key Figures of Moroccan E-commerce

- **25 million** active internet users in Morocco
- **+35%** online transactions compared to 2024
- **72%** of online purchases are made from a smartphone
- **CMI and mobile payment** growing strongly
- **Delivery**: Amana, Glovo and private carriers cover the entire territory

## Sectors Booming Online

### Fashion and Accessories

The fashion sector is the undisputed leader of Moroccan e-commerce. Both local and international brands are capitalizing on consumers' appetite for online fashion. Instagram boutiques transitioning to e-commerce see their sales triple on average.

### Beauty and Cosmetics

The online beauty market saw 45% growth in 2025. Moroccan consumers, especially women aged 25-40, are increasingly buying beauty products online, attracted by the variety and competitive prices.

### Food and Local Products

Moroccan local products (argan oil, honey, saffron, spices) are finding an international audience through e-commerce. It's a golden sector for entrepreneurs looking to export Moroccan expertise.

### Craftsmanship and Decoration

Moroccan craftsmanship — carpets, pottery, leather goods — attracts buyers from around the world. A well-designed e-commerce site can transform a local artisan into an international exporter.

## How to Launch Your Online Store in Morocco

### Step 1: Choose Your Niche

Don't sell everything to everyone. Focus on a sector you know and where you can offer real added value. A targeted catalog of 20-50 products is worth more than a generic catalog of 500 items.

### Step 2: Create Your E-commerce Site

You have two main options:

- **Turnkey solution** (Shopify, WooCommerce): quick to set up, but limited in customization
- **Custom development** (Next.js, React): more flexible, performant and adapted to your specific needs

At Ibda3 Digital, we recommend custom development for ambitious projects that want to stand out.

### Step 3: Integrate Payment

In Morocco, online payment options are multiplying:

- **CMI** (Centre Monetique Interbancaire): Visa/Mastercard bank cards
- **Mobile payment**: solutions like M-Wallet
- **Cash on delivery**: still preferred by 60% of Moroccan buyers
- **WhatsApp Commerce**: very popular for small shops

### Step 4: Organize Logistics

Delivery is the key to success. Several options are available:

- **Amana** (Barid Al-Maghrib): national coverage, affordable rates
- **Private carriers**: Glovo, InDrive, etc.
- **Own delivery**: for local areas (Marrakech, Casablanca)

### Step 5: Drive Traffic

A site without visitors doesn't sell. Your main levers:

- **SEO**: optimize your site for Google (keywords, content, technical)
- **Social media**: Instagram and TikTok are your best allies
- **Google Ads**: for immediate results on commercial queries
- **Influencer marketing**: collaborate with Moroccan influencers

## Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Neglecting mobile** — 72% of your customers will buy from their phone
2. **Ignoring cash on delivery** — it's still the preferred method
3. **Copying foreign sites** — adapt the experience to the Moroccan market
4. **Forgetting customer service** — WhatsApp and responsiveness are essential
5. **Underestimating photography** — quality product photos multiply conversions by 3
